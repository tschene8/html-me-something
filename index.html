A Series of Professional Events

Career path from college to present

My first job out of college was an assistant manager at Walgreens.


We called ourselves "glorified stockboys".
  1. We stocked shelves (obviously)
  2. We processed customer returns and exchanges
  3. We 'supervised' employees who thought they knew more than us... and quite possibly did

It was run of the mill retail management.

I left Walgreens and took a management position with FedexOffice.


There I dove into a world of Kinkos printing combined with Fedex shipping.
After a couple years I was promoted to store manager at my own location.

I was working up the ranks, but the print industry was quickly downsizing.

I found a supply chain position with the Department of Mental Health at Bellefontaine Habiliation Center.


I was able to jump right in and do what I'm good at: control inventory, make educated purchasing decisions, and save money.

A few of my duties included:

  • purchasing
  • inventory control
  • asset management
  • supervising the department food court
Unfortunately the position did not pay very much. I had to move on after less than a year.

A small business called Missouri Gold Buyers recruited me to become one of their store managers.


The job was what it sounds like... I bought gold from people who wanted to sell it.
I was brought in with promises of company growth and all the bells and whistles, within 6 months they closed two-thirds of their stores.
so that didn't work out.

I became a carrier with the United States Postal Service


When I took this position 3 years ago I felt like all my education and experience wasn't worth anything.
  • I drive a postal truck
  • I sort endless amounts of mail
  • I deliver mountains of packages
Although my postal duties are quite time consuming, I have always been used to more responsibility.
I decided to be the local shop steward at my office, proud member of the NRLCA.


NRLCA

The National Rural Letter Carriers' Association (NRLCA) is an American labor union that represents Rural letter carriers employed
by the United States Postal Service. The purpose of this Association shall be to "improve the methods used by rural letter carriers,
to benefit their conditions of labor with the United States Postal Service (USPS), and to promote a fraternal spirit among its members."
